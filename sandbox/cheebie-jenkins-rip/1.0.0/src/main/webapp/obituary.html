A.B. Jenkins, better known as Ms. Cheebie, was born to Mary Durbon and Sam Clark on July 7, 1922. She was born in Weaver, LA. Soon after, she moved to Many, LA. There, should would start her legacy. She believed in the Lord's grace and cohesive family values. Miss Cheebie embedded strong Christian values in her children, grandchildren, and greatgrandchildren. But she didn't just hold the lord's word to her family. Miss Cheebie blessed everyone with the lord's spirit. After the lord, Ms. Cheebie Jenkins consistently put her family on the front line. She was a strong Mother, a wise grandmother, and a loving and kind great-grandmother. She loved family gatherings. She also loved watching Wheel of Fortune, enjoyed Summer afternoons, and a good conversation with anyone. She loved to see the deepest side of a person. Miss Cheebie Jenkins had an open-door policy. Anyone was welcome. Her heart was truly filled with love. She was the glue that held the family together. She exemplified forgiveness, gentleness, understanding, wisdom about life's tribulations, and she was very easy to talk to. Her eyes were full of life. Her voice was flooded with knowledge and excitement. Her touch was softly definitive. Her life's story was a celebration of the righteous coming out on top by the lord's grace despite all life's challenges. Ms. Cheebie Jenkins has won. Her memory will live on forever in our hearts.

Preceding her in death, was her parents, 7 brothers, and 8 sisters. She leaves to cherish her memories great grandchildren Darnell Clark, Ella Lee Ann Clark, Joseph Michael Rice Jr, Jacobi Rice, Kiniah Ann Rice, Cyar Brown, Angel Elivares, Kenneth Ruffin, Ghrico Rice, Terron Rice, Gerrisha Rice, Jjhriko Rice, Jerron Rice, Jerrisha Rice, Adriana Marco, Yolando Marcho, Shawanna Sims, and Marlon Sims.
